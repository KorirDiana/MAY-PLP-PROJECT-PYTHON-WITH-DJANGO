This graph illustrates the carbon emissions associated with various shipping routes. Understanding these emissions is crucial for identifying areas for improvement in sustainability efforts.

Identifying Optimal Routes Cost-Benefit Analysis: Calculate total costs (fuel, time, emissions) for each route and compare. Emission Analysis: Determine the total CO2 emissions per route using regression coefficients from your model to predict emissions based on the route's characteristics. Sensitivity Analysis: Assess how changes in key variables (like speed or fuel prices) affect overall costs and emissions, allowing you to identify the most critical factors influencing route optimization. Scenario Planning: Use different hypothetical scenarios (e.g., changes in fuel prices, regulatory impacts) to see how routes perform under varying conditions. By leveraging these variables and methods, you can create a robust framework for predicting and optimizing ship routes for better efficiency and lower emissions.
